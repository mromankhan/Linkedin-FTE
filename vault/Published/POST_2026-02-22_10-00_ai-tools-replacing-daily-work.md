## Post Content

I used to spend 3 hours a day on tasks that AI now handles in 15 minutes.

Not because I found shortcuts. Because I stopped treating AI as a search engine and started treating it as a teammate.

Here are 5 tools that actually moved the needle:

**Cursor** — Writes 60% of my boilerplate. I focus on architecture, it handles the tedious parts.

**Perplexity** — Research that used to take 30 minutes now takes 5. Cited sources included.

**Notion AI** — Meeting notes, summaries, and action items generated automatically. Zero manual transcription.

**Claude** — Complex reasoning, writing drafts, debugging logic. My go-to for anything requiring real thinking.

**Zapier + AI actions** — Background workflows that run while I sleep. Emails sorted, data synced, follow-ups sent.

The real unlock isn't the tools themselves.

It's building workflows where they chain together. When Perplexity feeds into Claude, which outputs into Notion, which triggers a Zapier action — that's when hours disappear from your schedule.

Most people use AI as a one-off tool. The ones winning in 2026 use it as infrastructure.
